THE LORE OF KEK

🎮
BORN IN WARCRAFT
In World of Warcraft, when a Horde player typed LOL, Alliance players saw KEK. A glitch became a legend. The Orcish language birthed an internet phenomenon that would outlast the game itself.
🇰🇷
KOREAN ROOTS
Long before WoW, Korean gamers in StarCraft used ㅋㅋㅋ to represent stifled laughter — the "k" sound. In-game it appeared as kekeke. Two cultures, one eternal laugh.
🐸
THE GOD OF CHAOS
Kek is an ancient Egyptian deity of darkness and chaos, depicted as a frog-headed figure. 4chan merged the meme with the myth. Pepe became the modern avatar. Meme magic was declared real.
🍰
TOP KEK
In Turkish, kek means cake. When 4chan discovered the Turkish snack brand Topkek, chaos ensued. "Top kek" became shorthand for something extremely amusing — the highest tier of kek achievable.
📺
KEKW ERA
Kek evolved into KEKW — one of Twitch's most iconic emotes. The face of Spanish comedian Juan Joya Borja laughing uncontrollably became the universal symbol of internet hilarity across every platform.
